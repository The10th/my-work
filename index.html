Static Site Duplication

Pixel perfect comp (comprehensive layouts) production was one of my favorite projects to work on while being a student. Being able to develop a functional website from a wireframe/mockup/screenshot was extremely rewarding to me, and is a skill I still love constantly refining. Please click on the images to get a closer look!


Day 1 of my 12 week bootcamp, and I was given a .png screenshot of a simple mock blog site to reproduce. In 3 hours my classmates and I learned the very basics of HTML & CSS, then by the 6th hour of the first day I produced the above comp. Immediately after I completed the mock blog site, I started working on a bit more complicated of a screenshot to reproduce.

By hour 8 of day 1 I began to learn the subtleties and nuances of comp production. I liked taking a screenshot and making a coded version of it, at the same time I appreciated the work that went into the original design of the sites. As my work started getting more complex I started to see how cumbersome my code was becoming and started to work on concise/efficient code.

After a long 16 hour first day, I went home and fell asleep with dreams of pink, green, yellow, white, and blue text against a dark gray screen. At the beginning of day 2 we were given more complex screenshots to reproduce, of which I liked very much and it taught me how to use the interwebs as an immensely powerful resource for best practice tips and tricks.

6 hours into day 2 and I successfully completed two complex comp productions, and it felt great being able to take a picture of how a site should look, and reproduce it in code. More work came in the following weeks, and very little sleep, but this started my foundation and affinity towards front end development.
